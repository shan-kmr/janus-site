Format two · the showcase

The proof no customer, no device, no consent could accuse us of

The site’s hero is that transform, running live on real open data. Below it, a menu of proof modules — each a different public dataset made legible, each pointed at a buyer. The hero module carries the pitch.

Hero moduleInsurers · Reinsurers · Vision-Zero cities · fleets

The crash surface no postcode can see

~2M geocoded NYC collisions, divided by an Overture-built exposure denominator, resolved to 100 m cells and animated by hour-of-day and rain. Raw density just recovers “where the cars are”; normalising by exposure surfaces the genuinely dangerous places that aren’t merely busy — TNEDICCA’s “true risk,” from zero private data.

NYC Collisions + Overture → exposure-normalised H3-r10 surface → “this corner, not that ZIP” → sell the map.

ModuleRobotics · logistics

Where GPS lies

Tower density (OpenCellID) × street geometry → a surface of where a raw coordinate is trustworthy versus garbage in the urban canyon.

OpenCellID + roads → fix-uncertainty field → trust map.

ModuleRetail · CRE · QSR

The 15-minute catchment

Transit + footprints + commute data → per-building “who can reach this, which mode, what hour” — catchment that ignores admin lines.

GTFS + Overture + LODES → reachability → siting index.

ModuleParametric · utilities

The city breathes

Daytime workers vs. residents → population that swings 10× between 3am and 5pm, so risk denominators move with the clock.

LODES + ACS + Places → diurnal exposure → true denominator.

ModuleMobility · transit

Movement grammar

Public trip records → an origin-destination flow field and learned catchments — the shape of how a city actually moves.

TLC trips + tracts → OD tensor → flow embeddings.

The hinge — open with the flex, close with the gap:

“This crash-risk surface for all of New York was built from zero customer data, zero devices, zero consent.”

“It knows this corner is dangerous. It cannot know that your policyholder drives it at 5pm, in the rain, twice a day.”

Open data is the stage — a place’s static risk. Your consented device signal is the actor — who moves through it, when, how. “Run the same engine on your stream: identical features, but per-person, per-trip, live.”

Kept honest (the gap is the pitch): open crash/commute/POI data is aggregate and static — environmental risk, never individual exposure. No identity resolution, no consent provenance, no real-time. Those three are exactly what the device layer supplies — which is why the showcase proves the engine and the customer’s consented stream is the fuel.

Build path

What it takes, in order

  • The showcase hero, first (≈1–2 wks). NYC Collisions (h9gi-nx95, public domain) + Overture, H3-r10 in MapLibre, the ZIP-vs-cell toggle as the money shot. Ship it as one page in the Geo-2 theme — this is the sales asset that opens doors on its own.
  • The film, from the existing engine. Fork hero-narrative.html; add the SOLD/collapse beat and the decision beat; score it; export a 55s cut + reuse the loops on the site.
  • Attribution discipline. Public-domain / CDLA-Permissive / Apache lanes for anything productised; keep ODbL (OSM, Overture buildings) + CC-BY-SA (OpenCellID) to the public demo, where share-alike is fine.
  • One line under every module: “…now on your consented data.” The showcase is never the product — it’s the argument for the product.
